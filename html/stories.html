Placebo Plaza

Summary:

The Placebo Plaza is an extradimensional plane where the beliefs and imaginations of it's inhabitants - conscious or subconcious - are made true, inhabitants who can freely enter or exit from the real world through a gateway object of their choice as an avatar of their creation. This episodic animated web series explores what happens when reality seeps into escapism, it's a comedy, it's a drama, it's a psychological horror.

Status:

Only the premise has been established.

( The Tragedy of ) a Mind Exposed

Summary:

An exploration of both extremes of the Dunning-Kruger effect. Our protagonist starts out as cocky and naive, always thinking that their way of doing things at least works well enough for them, to everyone else's disappointment. At some point they learn to listen to what others can teach them, and they becomes more receptive to help. This however turns into not trusting their own judgement and belief that everyone else knows better than them about everything. In the finale, they try to wrestle back control and assert their cofidence and competence... in a task they have no experience in, which costs them their life.

Status:

Only the premise has been established.

Will You be Bamboozled??

Summary:

A classic "general knowledge" trivia show, but with a twist: some of the questions are outright impossible (or at least requires infinite time to fully answer), which contestants will have to opt out from. Scoring goes as follows: a point for a correct answer or correct opt out, no points for a wrong answer, and you lose a point for opting out of a question with a correct answer, and also for trying to answer an impossible question. The main draw of the show will be watching (however many) contestants floundering as they cautiously opt out of possible questions and impulsively answer impossible ones.

Status:

Only the premise has been established.

The Ever-changing Zeitgeist

Summary:

In a world much like our own, a person's fate in death is determined by the current Zeitgeist, the general beliefs and values of the population at the time. Would society think a person like you deserved paradise? Or damnation? Would you rather conform to the Zeigeist, or push for revolution in a bid to create a new one?

The Cassandra Show

Summary:

Cassandra of Troy was given the gift of prophecy by a god but because of... romantic reasons she was cursed so that none believed them, in spite of their truth. This series would attempt to replicate the feeling in the (real-life) audience, by writing a show so incomprehensible that not even multiple people in the same room would be able to describe it to each other, explanations ideally dissloving into fits of laughter and incoherent stammering.

Status:

Only the premise has been established.

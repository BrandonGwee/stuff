A top down turn based local / online board game where players create their own characters in a 32x32 pixel sprite creator.

  • 4 players are dropped into an 8x8 grid, everyone must decide their move before they all move at the same time. All characters can only move 1 space in one of the 4 cardinal directions (↑, ↓, ←, or →)
  • When 2 players interact with each other, they are placed in a combat minigame where they must hit their opponent's character with more force than their's is hit with. Characters move and attack by being thrown around (a proof-of-concept can be found here). Collisions will not cause damage if the character isn't moving fast enough, but it is at that speed when they can be clicked on and thrown again. When a battle is decided, the losing player's character is thrown backwards 3 tiles and one of their 3 hitpoints is deducted.
  • Items will appear every other turn, which players can either use on the board, or non-combatants can use as a disruption when the other players are battling.
  • Boards will have different themes and different hazards to match.
  • The game will need players to sign in and verify that they're 16 or older because at least 40% of sprites are not going to suitable for all ages and there's no way the team can moderate all of them. However the game will have a votekicking system where players can be forced out the match if the majority decides they should be. They will be replaced with a computer controlled character using a garbled, unrecognisable version of their sprite.

A rhythm game based on vinyl records.

  • The game uses a chart like Dance Dance Revolution or Guitar Hero The difference here being that notes rotate around on a circular disc, appearing at the 0° mark and having to be pressed as they complete one rotation around the disk. The disk takes 4 beats to complete 1 full rotation.
  • This game's main mechanic is taken from turntablists, who build their performance around altering the playback of their songs to produce new music, which is adapted into the game by the speed of the song changing, or the song being paused, or even reversing which requires previously hit notes to be hit again.
  • The game's levels are sorted into playlists, and each one culminates in a "Remix Track", a medley of all the songs in that track, with additional music changes to the existing tracks.

The project I would first suggest to a team: A microgame collection.

  • Microgames have very simple control schemes and only last about 4-5 seconds, that time being reduced after every few games.
  • The current most popular example of a microgame collection is the Warioware series of games, so most of the features listed here just plagiarise borrow elements as inspiration from that.
  • Microgames come in sets that all follow a theme (e.g. Sports, Fantasy). Each level features a random selection of microgames from that set, and in the Warioware games, culminates in a Boss microgame, which is longer and a bit more involved than the other games.
  • Players have a limited number of lives for each level, and the game ends either when all lives are lost, or if we implement boss microgames, this is the player's first playthrough of that level and the boss microgame is cleared. After the level is cleared once, it becomes a high-score-chaser and only running out of lives ends the game.
  • Each level has a character to host the games, and starts with framing sequence where the character tries to do something, with their success or failure correlating to the player's performance in the microgames.
